SafeEats – Owner Quick Start

Set up your restaurant, upload menus, and keep allergen information accurate.

What is SafeEats?

SafeEats helps restaurants manage menus with clear allergen and dietary information. You can add items manually, upload a menu (PNG/JPEG/PDF) to auto-generate editable items, and use the ingredient parser to detect allergens and dietary categories quickly.

What you’ll need

  • Access to the SafeEats web app (desktop browser recommended).
  • Restaurant details: name, address, phone, cuisine type.
  • Optional: a digital copy of your menu as PNG, JPEG, or PDF.

Create your account and access your restaurant

  1. Open the SafeEats app and select Register or Login.
  2. After logging in, create your restaurant profile if prompted (name, address, phone, cuisine).
  3. Owners can see their restaurant on the home screen. Select it to open the menu management page.
Tip: If you don’t see your restaurant, contact an administrator to confirm your owner access.

Add or update menu items

Option A: Upload your menu (recommended)

  1. Go to your restaurant’s menu page.
  2. Select Upload Menu and choose a PNG, JPEG, or PDF file.
  3. Wait for “Extracting items…” then review the generated item forms.
  4. Adjust names, descriptions, and prices as needed.

Option B: Add items manually

  1. Click Add Item.
  2. Enter Name, Description (optional), and Price.
  3. Paste an ingredients list (optional) to use the parser.
Ingredient parser: Paste ingredients and click Parse. SafeEats suggests allergens and dietary categories. You can toggle them on/off before saving.

Review allergens and dietary categories

  • Allergens include: milk, eggs, fish, tree nuts, wheat, shellfish, peanuts, soybeans, sesame.
  • Dietary categories include: vegan, vegetarian.
  • Use the toggles on each item to confirm or correct the suggested values.

Save changes

  1. Click Add on individual items, or use Add All Items to save in parallel.
  2. When complete, return to your restaurant’s page to confirm the updated list.
Best practice: After major menu updates, use the parser and do a quick allergen review to ensure accuracy.

Keeping data current

  • Update items whenever recipes or suppliers change.
  • Re-run the parser for new or reformulated items and double-check allergens.
  • Remove discontinued items to keep the customer view clean.

Troubleshooting

  • Can’t log in: Check your email and password or contact an admin.
  • Upload fails: Use PNG/JPEG/PDF under your system’s size limits. Try a single page if the file is large.
  • Parser results look off: Edit manually using the toggles—your changes take precedence.
